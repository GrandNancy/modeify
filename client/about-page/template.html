CarFreeAtoZ is an exciting new resource designed to give travelers a more personalized picture of their transportation options. Part of Arlington County's Transit Tech Initiative, CarfreeAtoZ aims to go beyond traditional approaches to journey planning, taking a more holistic view of connectivity and accessibility.

CarFreeAtoZ utilizes open transportation data standards such as OpenStreetMap and GTFS, as well as the OpenTripPlanner multimodal routing engine. The application currently covers walking, biking, and driving in the greater Washington/Baltimore area, as well as the following transit services: ART, DASH, DC Circulator, Fairfax Connector, MTA, PRTC, Ride-On, VRE, and WMATA. Additional transit providers will be added as data becomes available.

CarFreeAtoZ is currently in beta, and we are very interested to hear what you think about the application. If you have comments specific to a particular travel option, please use the built-in feedback link within that option's summary. We also encourage you to complete this survey about your overall experience using the site, and you may send any other feedback to feedback@carfreeatoz.com.
